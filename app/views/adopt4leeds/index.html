If you are interested in applying to adopt you should attend one of our adoption information events.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​

Come along to our next adoption information evening to find out more and have your questions answered. The evening will last approximately 2 hours and there's no need to book. At the end of the evening you can collect a request for initial home visit form which you should complete if you'd like to continue the adoption process with us.

Our adoption information evenings are presented by experienced members of our team. They will give you more information about adoption, describe how the application process works and tell you more about the support we provide. You will also hear from experienced adoptive parents about what it's like to adopt with us. It's a good opportunity to ask us any questions you may have - the evening will help you decide if adoption is right for you.

You should make sure you have read this website thoroughly before attending an Information Evening, especially the information about the children we currently need families for. As if you are unable to provide a home for the children who need adoption we may not be able to take up your application at this time.

Adoption Information Evening

​This information evening will take place on, Tuesday

21st March 2017

​At Leeds City Council offices, Kernel House, Killingbeck Drive, Leeds, LS14 6UF.

​Kernel House is located on the ASDA shopping estate on York Road, Seacroft. Car parking is available outside the building but spaces are limited. The venue is accessible from a variety of bus routes such as; 64/64A, 843, 56, 402, 840.

​To access Kernel House turn onto Killingbeck Drive from York Road and turn Left at the bend in the road. The car park is your second right. It can be misleading as the first building has fostering and adoption advertising on but this is an Adult Social Care Building so please do not park here.

​Please arrive for 6.30pm the evening will finish at approximately 8.30pm. There will be a formal presentation from social workers with a question and answer session and a film of Leeds adoptive parents.​​​​​​​
