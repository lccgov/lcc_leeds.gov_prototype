Children and young people of all ages need foster carers, and we’ll ask you if you have an age preference as part of your assessment.

We’re currently seeking foster carers for:

  • young people aged 11 -17
  • large sibling groups
  • children and young people who are disabled and/or have complex needs
  • parent and child
  • unaccompanied asylum seeking children

The different kinds of fostering we offer are:

  • Click to expand Short term

    This can range from an overnight stay up to two years. Carers provide a safe and secure place to live while we try to reunite the child with their family or find a permanent family.

    The age range of children can be from babies and infants up to teens.

  • Click to expand Long term

    This is usually for a number of years for children who can’t live at home. They’ll often have contact with their birth families and carers need to support contact.

    Long term fostering is usually for older children and they will stay with you until they leave care.

  • Click to expand Emergency

    Children can need a safe place in an emergency with no notice and at any hour. Carers look after a child briefly before they’re moved to another foster place or back to their families.

  • Click to expand Respite

    This is fostering for a specific period of time, usually from a weekend up to two weeks. Carers look after children whilst their own short or long term carers aren’t available or to give them a break.

    Children from different ages and with various needs will be looked after and you’ll be paid for the time you care for the child.

  • Click to expand Support care

    These provide support to children living at home with their families. Children will spend one to three overnight stays with a foster carer every few weeks. We’re particularly interested in households where there’s a male adult role model carer.

    Short breaks can be for both younger and older children.

  • Click to expand Caring for disabled children

    This is for children who may have physical, sensory or learning disabilities. Our team works with disabled children, their families and carers in supporting them in offering new experiences for the child.

    We are looking for people who are able to care for children with a wide range of additional needs including autism, learning disability, physical impairments and health needs. While we are recruiting carers for children with a range of needs, we would especially like to hear from people whose home can accommodate children who are wheelchair users (after adaption).

    We also need carers with at least one weekend every 6 weeks to share with a child for a short break. Our short breaks carers offer a fun environment for the child; an opportunity to enjoy new experiences and activities, to build new relationships, be included in a new family, and increase their independence.

  • Click to expand Supported lodgings

    The role is ideal for people who like to support teenagers but can’t commit to full time fostering.

    You will be able to provide a room and shared facilities for a young people aged 16 to 21 years old (25 if in further education or with additional needs) who has been in care or homeless. You will give emotional and practical support to help a teenager learn to live independently.

    Carers can work full time and are paid an allowance for every young person they take.

  • Click to expand Parent and child

    this involves showing a parent, usually young themselves, how to be a good parent and care for their child.

    A foster carers input helps decide whether the parent should be allowed to continue caring for their child or by somebody else.

  • Click to expand Teenager plus

    this is specific care for young people aged 13 or over. Foster carers will need to have experience in working with teenagers.

    Currently, there aren’t enough carers for young people and the number of teenagers needing care is increasing.

    If you’re interested in caring for young people, you’ll need to tell us this when you contact us.

  • Click to expand Rent a room

    TBC.

Please get in touch with us if you questions about any of the above types of fostering.
