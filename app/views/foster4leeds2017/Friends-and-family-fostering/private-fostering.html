    3. Private fostering

    Private fostering is when a child under 16 (or under 18 if disabled) is living for 28 days or more with someone who is not a close relative.

    This might be a friend, a great aunt, a cousin or someone else known to the child. If you are a close relative looking after a child this is known as Kinship care.

    A close relative is defined as a grandparent, aunt, uncle, brother or step-parent by marriage.

    Many parents who’ve made informal arrangements for friends or extended family to care for their child don’t realise it’s private fostering.

    Warning

    To keep vulnerable children safe and support families, by law parents and carers must notify their local authority if they have a private fostering arrangement.

    Examples of private fostering

    A private fostering arrangement might be put in place for a child or young person when:

    • parents work or study long and/or anti-social hours
    • they are sent to the UK for education or health care by birth parents from outside the UK
    • they live with a friend's family because of their parents’ separation, divorce or other difficulties at home
    • a teenager lives with their partner's family
