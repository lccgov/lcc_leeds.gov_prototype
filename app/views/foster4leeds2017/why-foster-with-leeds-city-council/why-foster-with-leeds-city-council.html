Fostering makes a real difference to children and young people and no special qualification is needed.

We know that you’ll need help and support and we offer this throughout your fostering journey.

Training

You’ll have lots of opportunities to access training to develop your skills in supporting children and young people through difficult times.

You can:

  • attend courses with all travel expenses paid
  • gain qualifications such as NVQ level 3
  • complete online training

All of these opportunities can work around your childcare commitments.

The weekly allowance per child is:

Weekly allowance per child
Childs age Amount
0 – 4 years £142.86
5 – 10 years £162.73
11 – 15 years £202.58
16 years plus £246.44

The payment for skills fee levels are:

Payment for skills fee level
Level First child Second child Each subsequent child Paid annual leave entitlement
1 £120 £120 £60 7 days
2 £170 £170 £85 10 days
3 £220 £220 £110 14 days

The allowances are examples and are subject to change.

Your level of skills will be discussed in detail at the home visit stage. We’ll advise you on how your experience are matched to a particular skills level.

Local support

Our fostering teams provide local support groups for our foster carers. These support groups include:

  • your own social worker
  • membership to the Leeds Foster Carer Association
  • membership to the Fostering Network
  • opportunities for you to meet other carers
  • the latest information on the work of the team and the services we provide
  • practical advice and support

Additional benefits

We also offer:

  • free annual Bodyline membership for foster carers
  • free annual junior Bodyline membership for children aged 8-18 years
  • free junior swimming lessons
  • discounts to local businesses
  • additional home and contents insurance for our approved foster carers

If you are already a foster carer registered elsewhere you can transfer to Leeds city council.
